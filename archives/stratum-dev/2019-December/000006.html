On Wed, Dec 11, 2019 at 3:44 PM Brian O'Connor <bocon at opennetworking.org>
wrote:

> Hi everyone,
>
> I am happy to announce the availability of TestVectors.
>
> There are two parts:
>
>    1. A set of initial test cases (called TestVectors) for bmv2, Broadcom
>    Tomahawk, and Barefoot Tofino targets:
>    https://github.com/stratum/testvectors
>    2. A runner that can be used to put a target switch under test:
>    https://github.com/stratum/testvectors-runner
>
> We will be working to integrate TestVectors into Stratum CI system to
> enable system test coverage of software and hardware switches. TestVectors
> are also useful vehicles for operators to formally specify requirements.
>
> To learn more, you can watch a few talks on TestVectors:
>
>    - Introduction and Rationale  (Alireza Ghaffarkhah & Tomek Madejski)
>    https://vimeo.com/307167637/e1117a14ac
>    - Architecture and Implementation Details (Abhilash Endurthi & You
>    Wang)
>    https://vimeo.com/361225907 (slides
>    <https://www.opennetworking.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/3.00pm-Abhilash-Endurthi-Test-Vectors.pdf>
>    )
>
> If you have any questions or want to contribute, please join the *#test*
> channel on the Stratum Slack <https://stratumproject.slack.com> (or invite
> yourself <https://slackin.stratumproject.org/> if you aren't already on
> Slack). You can also direct questions at the dev mailing list: *stratum-dev at lists.stratumproject.org
> <stratum-dev at lists.stratumproject.org>*
>
> Finally, a big thank you to Ali, Devjit, Tomek and team at Google for
> kicking things off, and to Abhilash and You at ONF for bringing this to
> open source.
>
> Thanks!
> Brian
